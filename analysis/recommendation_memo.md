# Task 1: Business Problem Statement

The company has introduced a new onboarding and activation campaign for its subscription-based digital product. Users were randomly assigned to either a Control group (existing onboarding experience) or a Treatment group (new campaign experience). Management must decide whether the new onboarding experience should be rolled out to all users.

This decision impacts multiple stakeholders, including the Product Team, Marketing Team, Customer Success Team, Revenue Team, and end users. A successful rollout should improve business performance while maintaining a positive customer experience.

The primary metric expected to improve is the Paid Conversion Rate, which measures the percentage of users who convert from free or trial users to paying customers. Improvements in related metrics such as onboarding completion, engagement, and revenue are also important indicators of success.

However, potential risks must be carefully monitored before making a launch decision. These risks include increased refund requests, higher support ticket volumes, lower engagement quality, longer conversion times, and poor performance among specific user segments. An increase in these guardrail metrics could indicate that the new experience is negatively affecting user satisfaction or revenue quality.

Before recommending a full rollout, evidence is required from experiment analysis and statistical testing. The Treatment group must demonstrate a meaningful and statistically significant improvement in the primary success metric without causing unacceptable deterioration in key guardrail metrics. The final recommendation will be based on conversion performance, business impact, statistical significance, and overall risk assessment.
