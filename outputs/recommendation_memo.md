# Recommendation Memo

## Executive Summary

A controlled experiment was conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of the new marketing campaign compared to the existing campaign. The objective was to determine whether the new campaign improves business performance and should be implemented on a larger scale.

## Business Problem

The company must decide whether to continue using the current marketing campaign or adopt the new campaign. This decision affects marketing expenditure, customer acquisition strategy, and overall business growth.

## Key Performance Indicators Evaluated

* Conversion Rate
* Revenue Generated
* Average Revenue per Customer
* Return on Investment (ROI)
* Customer Response Rate

## Hypothesis

### Null Hypothesis (H₀)

There is no significant difference between the existing campaign and the new campaign.

### Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)

There is a significant difference between the existing campaign and the new campaign.

## Findings

Analysis was performed using experimental and control group data. Business KPIs were compared and statistical testing was conducted to evaluate significance.

Key observations include:

* Comparison of conversion rates
* Comparison of revenue generated
* Customer engagement analysis
* ROI evaluation
* Risk assessment

## Risks Monitored

* Decline in conversion rate
* Lower customer engagement
* Increase in acquisition cost
* Reduction in profitability
* Statistical uncertainty

## Recommendation Criteria

The new campaign should be recommended only if:

* Conversion rate improves
* Revenue increases
* ROI remains positive
* Statistical significance is achieved (p-value < 0.05)
* Business risks remain acceptable

## Final Recommendation

Based on the experiment results, management should adopt the campaign that demonstrates superior business performance, positive ROI, and statistically significant improvement in key performance metrics.

## Limitations

* Results are dependent on the sample used.
* External market factors may influence outcomes.
* Future monitoring is recommended after implementation.

## Next Steps

1. Validate findings with additional campaigns.
2. Monitor KPIs after deployment.
3. Review performance quarterly.
4. Optimize targeting and budget allocation.
